Ongoing Research Projects

Quantum Materials

We research a wide range of materials that exhibit quantum phenomena. In particular some of the characteristics of interest are:

  • Spins and electrons in low dimensions
  • Frustrated magnets
  • Semiconductors with strong spin-orbit coupling
  • Quantum phase transitions



Quantum Defects

NV centers in diamond are a reliable way to measure magnetic fields with nanoscale precision. However, diamonds can be hard to fabricate and at times are difficult to interface with the samples under study. Our goals are to develop materials for solid state quibit based on color centers while using new form factors and materials. Some paths we are researching are:

Shallow NVs

ESR of surface spins, local probe of optically-inactive spins in materials

2D materials

Easy interface with quantum materials, strain/pressure/temp sensing in membranes

Nanotubes

NMR of solutions/materials confined within nanotubes, scanning nanotube probe
